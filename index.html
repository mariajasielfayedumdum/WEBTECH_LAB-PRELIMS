Why talk about Gender Equality?

Gender equality is the view and treatment that everyone should receive without any discrimination based on gender. We often talked about it when it comes to power, decision- making, employment, gender-based violence, freedom, gender stereotypes, harmful traditional practices, portrayal of women in the media, health, rights, education and gender mainstreaming. But every time we talk about gender equality, we always take the women's side not minding the men's side which will balance the understanding of the said topic. The countries in Europe has compiled a Gender Equality Glossary, drawn from international and regional women's human rights instruments. Also, last January 21, 2017, during the newly-inaugurated President of the United States, Donald Trump, hundreds of thousands of women marched on Washington to fight for their rights and to stop sexual harassment. This is an example of women fighting for themselves.

There are a lot of issues when it comes to equal treatments and the rights of women. One issue is the cultural factors like "Patrilocality". It is when a woman gets married, she essentially ceases to be a member of her birth family and joins her husband's family. Under this system, parents potentially reap more of the returns to investments in a son's health and education because he will remain a part of their family, whereas a daughter will physically and financially leave the household upon marriage. Based on these cultural factors, women don't really have the right to choose the life she wants to live. It's like they were made just to take care of their children and stay at home. Also, when it comes to education, parents would prefer to let their son continue studying instead of their daughter because they'll know that one day, she will stay at home and will waste all the knowledge she learned.

The 1987 Philippine Constitution affirms the equality of men and women before the law and recognizes the role of women in nation-building. Despite these constitutional mandates, much remains to be done before our society truly breaks down all forms of gender barriers and attains gender equality as envisioned in our Charter. This issue of the Philippine Law Journal is a step towards that direction. It offers scholarly discussions on relevant and timely gender issues such as discrimination, domestic violence and reproductive rights.

You see, educating and training our minds with the right information and knowledge will help us to be a better individual. We shouldn't be criticizing each other's inabilities to do things, thus, we should start to help each other, build each other. That's what humanity is for. Imagine if we won't stop this right now, what will happen on the future generations? Things will get worse as time goes by. Change starts within us, and let's be the change we want to see in the world.

About

We, the 3rd year students of Saint Louis University, created this website to advocate for and raise awareness about Gender Equality. Our site aims to promote the importance of gender equality in education and open the eyes of the readers about gender equity. We promote gender equality to reduce extreme poverty and boost shared prosperity for girls and boys, women and men, across the world. Gender equality is a major problem affecting people all over the world. Unfortunately, not everyone is aware of this problem. Promote gender equality by eliminating gender disparity in society. By raising awareness we can move a little closer to achieve the independence and an equal education opportunity for boys and girls. Another goal of this website is to change the point of view of each and everyone of us through things, to widen our perspective in life, and to make everyone understand that each of us should be treated the way we should be treated. This campaign not only focuses on women's rights, but also the issues that conservatives in the legislature would consider taboo, including divorce, abortion, and the rights of lesbians, gays, bisexuals and transgenders (LGBT).
